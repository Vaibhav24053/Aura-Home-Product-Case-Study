The Gap Nobody
Is Filling

Indian homeowners want beautiful spaces — but face a brutal binary choice. Hire an interior designer, or browse Pinterest for months with nothing actionable at the end. There is no intelligent middle ground that is free, personalised, and India-specific. AuraHome is that middle ground.

02 — MARKET RESEARCH

Four Gaps.
Zero Competitors.

Before writing a single line of code, a market scan revealed four clear opportunities that every existing player had missed — all four became core features of AuraHome.

GAP 1
No Free AI Room Visualizer for India
Modsy and Roomvo are US-centric, paid, and show Western furniture only. Indian consumers need Indian aesthetics with Indian product links. Nobody built this.
GAP 2
Vastu + Modern Design Has No Digital Home
Vastu consultation costs Rs.5,000+ per session or gets buried in YouTube videos. An AI that blends Vastu principles with contemporary aesthetics simply doesn't exist anywhere.
GAP 3
No Community for Real Budget Transformations
Instagram shows aspirational rooms without context, creating paralysis. No platform lets real people share 'I did this transformation' with before/after photos and honest numbers.
GAP 4
Design Personality Is Completely Unexplored
Users don't know what they want until they see it. A quiz-based design archetype gives users a shareable framework — and creates a built-in viral content loop.

08 — KEY LEARNINGS

What This Build
Actually Taught Me

Four non-obvious lessons from a weekend build that shipped a full-stack AI product. Not theory — observed from doing.

01
Vibe coding is a real product skill
Using Replit AI Agent with structured prompts, a non-developer can ship a full-stack product in a weekend. The skill is knowing WHAT to build and WHY — the AI handles HOW. This is the future of product prototyping. The bottleneck is no longer writing code; it's product clarity.
02
India-specific = automatic differentiation
Adding Vastu-Modern, INR pricing, and Indian store names cost zero extra dev time but created a product no competitor offers. Localisation is the cheapest moat available. Most builders default to English-US because it's easier — that's the gap to own.
03
The quiz is the best acquisition feature
Shareable personality results drive organic growth with zero paid marketing spend. A quiz onboarding also solves blank-canvas paralysis — users arrive with a design identity before they touch any tool. It turns a cold start into a warm first session.
04
Reliability beats free when it matters
Switching from Gemini's free tier to GPT-4o-mini was the right call. A working prototype is worth more than a broken one that runs on zero budget. Knowing when to spend and when to save is a core builder skill — not a compromise.
05
Experimenting across tools sharpens your judgment
Beyond Replit AI Agent, I also experimented with OpenAI Codex and Antigravity to compare code and UI outputs across all three tools. Each has a different mental model — Codex excels at precise code generation, Antigravity at rapid UI iteration, and Replit AI Agent at full-stack end-to-end building. The real skill is knowing which tool to reach for at which stage of the build.

⚠️ Prototype Disclaimer — Please Read

Generated room images may not accurately reflect your real space. This is a prototype — not a production-grade application. Using a general-purpose image generation API (GPT-Image-1) means the AI interprets your room from a single photo and applies a style prompt. Results are directional and inspirational, not pixel-perfect reconstructions.

To generate truly accurate, room-specific outputs — where the AI understands your exact dimensions, furniture positions, lighting, and textures — would require training custom diffusion models on large datasets of room images, significant ML engineering effort, and a dedicated team of engineers, designers, and domain experts working full time. That is a different product at a different scale.

The same applies to other features — the budget planner, Vastu recommendations, and community tools are functional prototypes built to validate ideas, not polished production features. High-quality, reliable outputs across every feature would require a full engineering team and extended runway.

This is a case study and a learning exercise — proof that the concept is valid, the market gap is real, and the user need is clear. It may evolve into a full-time product in the future. For now, it is an honest, working prototype that tells a story worth telling.

"This case study is proof that modern product building doesn't require a dev team, a VC cheque, or months of runway. It requires a clear problem, the right tools, and the product thinking to make the right call at every step. AuraHome is a prototype — but it works, it ships, and it tells a story."
